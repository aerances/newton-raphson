NumericalMethods // Newton-Raphson

1. Core Mechanics

The Newton-Raphson scheme locates real roots of differentiable profiles where $f(x) = 0$ by using local tangent vectors sequentially.

Linear Taylor Setup

Expanding your current iteration threshold point through a localized first-order Taylor sequence assumes a small step variance $h = r - x_0$:

$$0 \approx f(x_0) + h \cdot f'(x_0)$$

The Operational Recurrence

Isolating for adjustment factor $h$ and implementing it across endless cycles changes our target point into an active sequence generator formula:

$$x_{n+1} = x_n - \frac{f(x_n)}{f'(x_n)}$$

Geometry Breakdown

Every step projects along the local function tangent point $(x_n, f(x_n))$. The zero-crossing point where this tangent line cuts cleanly across the $x$-axis sets the stage for your next point entry vector.

Anomalous Convergence: Stationary regions where $f'(x_n) \to 0$ will crash the computation matrix completely or shift the vector trajectory out to infinity.
